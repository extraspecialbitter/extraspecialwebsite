I have gone to HR for hostile work environment issues and it is not easy or fun, but if you feel it is the right thing to do than it should be done.

For legitimate issues (I am assuming that is the case you are in) it is illegal if the company retaliates against you, such as firing you for going to HR. This is not to say that your life will be great. Don't be surprised if you get watched for and called on for little things that where Ok before but really stretched some lines. People are evil.

You want to make sure that the story you give them is documented and that they have a copy and you do too. If there are specifics give dates of when things happened. A lot of places don't have official policies on what needs to happen when a complaint is submitted. Most likely the person will be spoken too and hopefully that will create change. If not you will need to go again. Find out what the company policy is and if you are going to be spoken about by name to the person in question.

Can't tell if you are "Crazy" as I don't know the issues.

As for a lawyer nothing legally has been done "wrong". From what you have said here it is one person's words and how they make another feel. If there is lose of a job or the Boss is breaking known company policy then the lawyer might be needed. For now sounds like HR is the right place to start.
