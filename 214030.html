the bad part 2

I think I'm getting older, wiser and less willing to fight back as I feel it is futile.

The background.

I started at my job almost two years ago, a newly formed department with a first time manager.
Two months after I started "we" interviewed our third to the group and it was a good fit.
Two months later our boss left and P and I worked out how to handle the work of 4 as we were planning to staff up beforehand.  Both of us did not want the manager position, both were offered it and in the end he was made interim.
Here is where I am going to stress what I've done good and bad because of where things are at now.
This is not meant as a "I'm awesome or I'm not to blame" but simply what I did as part of my job and what work means to me.
Because of security issues and training, I stayed late every Thursday and Friday with P. to make sure any access he needed was there and to help train him on our systems.  This was while doing my own work.  Basically we work late every Thursday and Friday due to the nature of fiancial institutions and production changes.
 I also worked every other and then every Friday night for one software project, 3 hours then 4 and finally 5 hours every Friday.
It ended when I told them to back out an implementation because it had failed and was told no.  I went to our SVP and asked that they take over their shit.  He agreed and there was no issue with my having fallen asleep at 2am waiting for someone to push a button.

Well as far as I knew all was going well.
We had a large long term project, a real pain in the ass deal that P took on.  I did my best to take *all* the remaining work
from him, and the number of helpdesk tickets and production tickets shows I did just that.  And finally we get a new boss.
I wasn't included in the interview process as P worked with him before.  I was told he was a good guy and that he had been out of work for 3 years.  Now I know why.

The first day I met him he referred to an incident with two cops in a car as two Fags blowing each other.  This was how I met my new boss.  I kept my mouth shut.

When did things go wrong?

As is known right after my neck surgery I got pneumonia that hid the MRSA staff infection.  So for two weeks I was running 100+ temps, that mixed with my Lyrica caused me to have three pseudo blackouts.  It wasn't until the 3rd one that my coworkers actually called my wife and had her come get me.

By this time we had just hired K, so his exposure to me was a week of me being sick.
From there I was out for 14 weeks fighting to stay alive.  A bit dramatic but not much.

It is typical to blame the guy who leaves for whatever was broken or not how you would like it or for fucks sake the color of the sky.

My boss complained multiple times about how much paperwork and yelling was involved in handling my disability with the back office.  He went so far as to refer to Duane the woman handling my claim as one of those southern niggers.  He actually said this not only to me but to Shazza.

So I finally return to work and am told I have a list of probationary issues to be aware of, that he didn't bring it up with H.R. before but that would change going forward.  He presented it as a clean slate and honestly I was just glad to be back to work.

So today I am talking to K and what I got was the following.

1: P before leaving claimed that he carried the department and felt that he did all the work.
2: That I was unreliable in the past as far as 24x7 oncall support.  To my credit I know of only one instance where I was unable to respond to an alert, but it was dealt with before start of business day.
3: That I should be aware of the perception people have of me, with a distinct flare that it was bad.
4: That since I was gone the department has been changing direction and that my skillset  needed to be adjusted.


This has left me with the feeling that while I was gone, because P left and both my Boss and K has to scramble to learn the job that I was the easy target for blame.  I was actually given an example of what I did wrong that I didn't even own as a project. 

I informed K that I was not going to badmouth P as that is not my way and that if he had that view it was his and my work doesn't reflect that and the numbers don't either.  Apparently the awards I had gotten which were sizable cash for work on projects and my bonus mean nothing.

All-in-all I feel like I should just fucking leave.  This coworker K, took less money to come  here because he wanted to use it as a step in, after only being there 4 months I heard him ask our boss about looking into other departments and was told he had to wait a year.

I just don't know what to do.  Well I did call my recruiter am updating my resume and he has a lead at Children's Hospital that would be similar pay so I do have a thought.

But I want to scream, fight, punch and beat these people.  I feel like I have put more effort and time into this department which I helped start and now I'm the unreliable bad guy with no work ethic.

As Shazza will tell anyone, I've worked from my hospital bed both after my gastric bypass, and years later after I lost 5 pints of blood while on a job site.  I worked from the hospital and when I was unable to drive after due to such a low red blood cell level was driven by Shazza to my coworker so they could bring me two job sites.  That's how frakking dedicated I am to what I do.

And here is the catch.  Is it worth fighting?  Is it worth going to H.R. which is in N.C?  Is it safe to talk to the SVP and explain how I feel.  I feel like saying "Look if you want me gone, fine, give me 3 months severence and I'll go."  I also feel like calling the family attorney and suing over a hostile work place.  I just hate it hate it hate it.

I know where my strengths are.  I know where I've fallen down, but I feel outnumbered.  I have a boss who couldn't find work for 3 years and it was only because of P that he got the job.  A boss whos is rude, homophobic and racist things I hate and had a subordinate of mine said those would have been disciplined.  And then I have the Marine K, who has now mustered down to reserves but thinks us being in Iraq is a good thing and actually snapped at me the first week I was back because I offered to help when he locked himself out of our monitoring system.

I just don't get it.

Tell me it's me, but tell me as you get older the desire to fight doesn't fade into apathy and a feeling that you won't win so don't try.  Is this what women go through now and then over maternity?  They had to keep me on but damn it I'm gonna pay for it?

Sadly I just don't see anywhere there to turn, like I said our HR isn't even in the state and I have no idea what the SVP even thinks of me.

Anyone have thoughts, good, bad, or just to tell me to suck it up?

First off, I sympathize. I even empathize. I have been in similar positions in my career, one involving a man whose name really was Mr. Payne.

And here's a duck-billed platitude which I believe will actually help. I've had to remind drwex of this more than once. Heck, I've had to remind myself of this more than once:
You can judge yourself by your work; you have control over that.

You cannot judge yourself by your job; you do not have control over that.

Only you know enough details of the situation to know whether it is worthwhile to suck it up. Only you know whether the situation at your job will resolve or even evolve into something better. And only you can determine whether there are things about it that you can fix.

But from what you've written here, it sounds like the answer is to go.

If you do go, I strongly recommend that you not burn bridges, as you never know when you'll be working with some of these same people again later in your career. P, for example, sounds like one of your references for your next job.
